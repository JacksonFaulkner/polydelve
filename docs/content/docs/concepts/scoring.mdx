## How scoring works

When a contract resolves (YES or NO), each user who held that contract receives a score update based on prediction accuracy.

Scoring lives in `features/probability.py`. The key inputs:

- **EPSS at purchase time** — what the market implied at the moment the user bought in
- **Resolution outcome** — YES (CVE landed) or NO (expired)
- **Calibration** — users who bought high-EPSS contracts that resolved YES score less than users who spotted low-EPSS packages that turned out to be exploited

This incentivizes finding underpriced risk, not just betting on packages that already look dangerous.

## Leaderboard

The leaderboard ranks users by cumulative accuracy score across all resolved contracts. It is displayed in the `LeaderboardTable` component and refreshed on page load.

Users who have not had any contracts resolve do not appear on the leaderboard.

## Schmeckle balance vs score

Schmeckle balance and leaderboard score are separate:

- **Balance** — economic position; reflects profit/loss on contracts
- **Score** — accuracy metric; reflects calibration quality regardless of bet size

A user can be a good predictor (high score) while being down Schmeckles if they consistently bet small on correct calls.
